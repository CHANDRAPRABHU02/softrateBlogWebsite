CyberSecurity

Cyber security is preparing for what’s next, not what was last. Cybersecurity may be fought with technology, but it is people who triumph. We must invest in the future generations of professionals who will carry on the fight.



What cybersecurity ??

Cybersecurity is the science of protecting networks, computers, programsanddata from attack, damage or unauthorized access.

  • Knowing what to do ---- science of cybersecurity
  • Knowing how to do ---- engineering of cybersecurity

Defining cybersecurity more technically “It is a ability to protect or defend the use of cyberspace from cyberattacks.”

Cyberspace: It is a global domain within the information environment consisting of the interdependent network of information systems infrastructure including the Internet, computer systems, telecommunication networks.


MAJOR SECURITY PROBLEMS:

  • Virus
  • Hacker
  • Trojan horses
  • Malware
  • Password cracking


VIRUS:

A computer virus is a malicious program loaded into a user’s computer without knowledge and runs against your wish. These virus are self-replicating and also designed to infect other programs.

To prevent our system getting affected by the virus install a security that protects the computer against threats such as viruses and worms.



HACKER:

Somebody who gains unauthorized access to computer systems or networks illegally, or in other words a hacker is a person who breaks into a system or network without any authorization to destroy, steal sensitive data from computer or perform malicious attacks. Usually a hacker would have enough knowledge to discover vulnerabilities in a target system. He/ She is generally a well skilled engineer or programmer who enjoys learning various programming languages and computer systems.

Types of hacker:

  • White Hat Hacker
  • Grey Hat Hacker
  • Black Hat Hacker


WHITE HAT HACKER:

  • They are considered as good guys because they follow the rules when it comes to hacking.
  • People whospecialized hacking check the faults of the systems.
  • In other words ,white hat hackers are the opposite of the black hat hackers. They’re the “ethical hackers”, experts in compromising computer security systems who use their abilities for good, ethical, and legal purposes rather than bad, unethical, and criminal purposes.

GREY HAT HACKERS:

  • They are both black and white hat
  • These type of hackers do not work for their own gain or to cause carnage but technically commit crimes and do arguably unethical things.
  • Grey hats might not use their access for bad purposes but they still compromise cyber security without permission ,which is illegal.
  • We cannot predict their behavior because sometimes they use their skills for the common good while in some other times he uses them foe their personal gains.

BLACK HAT HACKERS:

  • Black hat hackers are called as “cybercriminals”.
  • They are malicious hackers who break the law to achieve their goals.
  • These hackers penetrate systems without permission to exploit known or zero -day vulnerabilities.
  • Black hat hackers break into secure networks to destroy data or make the network unusable for those who are authorized to use the network.

TROJAN HORSES:

  • Trojan horses are email viruses that can duplicate themselves ,steal information , or harm the computer system.
  • These viruses are the most serious threats to computers.
  • It is a type of malicious code or software that looks legitimate but can take control of your computer.
  • These trojan’s are designed in such a way that to damage ,disrupt, steal , or in general inflict some other harmful action on your data or network.
  • To avoid trojans ,Security suites, such as Avast Internet Security, will prevent you from downloading Trojan Horses.

MALWARE:


  • The word “Malware” comes from the term “MALicious softWARE.”
  • Malware is any software that infects and damages a computer system without the owner’s knowledge or permission.
  • To stop malware ,Download an anti-malware program that also helps to prevent infections.
  • Activate Network Threat Protection, Firewall, Antivirus.

TYPES OF MALWARE:

  1. Ransomware
  2. Spyware
  3. Adware
  4. Worms
  5. Trojans
  6. Viruses

RANSOMWARE:



  • The most dangerous of all, Ransomware is a type of malware which encrypts the file stored in the target’s computer and demands a fee or ransom to decrypt the same.
  • Strong defense system needs to be developed including healthy browsing habits because threats of ransomware is increasing day by day.
  • It cannot be unlocked without paying the ransom, because the encryption is extremely strong .

SPYWARE:

  • Spyware is a common type of malware which monitors the activities performed by acomputer user on his or her PC.
  • The main intension of a spyware is to collect the private information of the computer user.
  • These spyware are downloaded by the users accidentally while downloading any free software from the internet.
  • These spyware is not only capable of transmitting private information but also change your computer settings and throw pop up ads.

ADWARE:

  • Adware targets internet users showing them different types of irrelevant ads.
  • These adware usually slow down your pc to a great extent, but no personal data is stolen by an adware.
  • It could also add vulnerabilities to your computer that could be exploited , and at times, it can collect and send your browsing history to third parties without your consent.
  • It can also trick you to installing a real malware using its ad network.

WORMS:

  • A type of virus, worms can enter your system through an email attachment.
  • These worms are self-replicating virus and can spread very quickly via computer network.
  • Computer worms destroys data and can use your PC to circulate spams.

TROJANS:

Trojan horse is a malware which appears to be a legitimate file. Trojan can make your PC slow and non-functional and compromises security. It has the ability to grant remote access of the user’s computer when it infects a PC. Entering a computer, it performs various tasks like corrupting files and sending out personal information.

PASSWORD CRACKING:

  • Password attacks are attacks by hackers that are able to determine passwords or find passwords to different protected electric areas and social network sites.
    • Securing Password:
    • Use always strong password
    • Never use same password for two different sites.
